# Task 01 – Wave Properties

## Problem Statement

A sound wave in air has a frequency of $440 \text{ Hz}$. If the speed of sound in air is $343 \text{ m/s}$, determine its wavelength in air. Then determine its wavelength in water, where the speed of sound is $1482 \text{ m/s}$.

## Theory

For a periodic wave, the relation between wave speed $v$, frequency $f$, and wavelength $\lambda$ is

$$
v = f \lambda
$$

Therefore,

$$
\lambda = \frac{v}{f}
$$

When a wave passes from one medium to another, its frequency remains unchanged, while its speed and wavelength may change.

## Step-by-Step Solution

Given:

$$
f = 440 \text{ Hz}
$$

### Wavelength in air

The speed of sound in air is

$$
v_{\text{air}} = 343 \text{ m/s}
$$

Using

$$
\lambda_{\text{air}} = \frac{v_{\text{air}}}{f}
$$

substitution gives

$$
\lambda_{\text{air}} = \frac{343}{440} \text{ m}
$$

$$
\lambda_{\text{air}} \approx 0.780 \text{ m}
$$

### Wavelength in water

The speed of sound in water is

$$
v_{\text{water}} = 1482 \text{ m/s}
$$

Using the same formula,

$$
\lambda_{\text{water}} = \frac{v_{\text{water}}}{f}
$$

$$
\lambda_{\text{water}} = \frac{1482}{440} \text{ m}
$$

$$
\lambda_{\text{water}} \approx 3.37 \text{ m}
$$

## Final Result

The wavelength in air is

$$
\lambda_{\text{air}} \approx 0.780 \text{ m}
$$

The wavelength in water is

$$
\lambda_{\text{water}} \approx 3.37 \text{ m}
$$

## Interpretation

The frequency is determined by the source and therefore remains the same in both media. Water transmits sound much faster than air, so the wavelength in water is much larger.
