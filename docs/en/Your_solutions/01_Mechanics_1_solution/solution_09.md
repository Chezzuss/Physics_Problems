# Task 09 – Momentum Comparison

## Problem Statement

Determine which object has greater momentum:

- a $2$-gram fly flying at $10 \text{ m/s}$,
- a $60$-gram tennis ball moving at $1 \text{ m/s}$.

## Theory

Linear momentum is defined as

$$
p = mv
$$

where:

- $m$ is the mass,
- $v$ is the speed.

To compare the momentum of two objects, masses must first be converted into SI units.

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Convert masses to kilograms

Fly:

$$
2 \text{ g} = 0.002 \text{ kg}
$$

Tennis ball:

$$
60 \text{ g} = 0.060 \text{ kg}
$$

---

### 2. Compute the fly's momentum

$$
p_{\text{fly}} = mv
$$

$$
p_{\text{fly}} = 0.002 \cdot 10
$$

$$
p_{\text{fly}} = 0.020 \text{ kg m/s}
$$

---

### 3. Compute the tennis ball's momentum

$$
p_{\text{ball}} = mv
$$

$$
p_{\text{ball}} = 0.060 \cdot 1
$$

$$
p_{\text{ball}} = 0.060 \text{ kg m/s}
$$

---

### 4. Compare the results

$$
0.060 > 0.020
$$

Therefore, the tennis ball has greater momentum.

The ratio is

$$
\frac{p_{\text{ball}}}{p_{\text{fly}}} = \frac{0.060}{0.020} = 3
$$

So the tennis ball's momentum is three times larger.

## Final Result

The tennis ball has the greater momentum.

Its momentum is

$$
0.060 \text{ kg m/s}
$$

while the fly's momentum is

$$
0.020 \text{ kg m/s}
$$

## Interpretation

Although the fly moves much faster, its mass is very small.

Momentum depends on both mass and speed, and in this comparison the larger mass of the tennis ball dominates. This is why the tennis ball has the greater momentum despite moving more slowly.
