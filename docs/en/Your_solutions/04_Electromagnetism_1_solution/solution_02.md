# Task 02 – Electric Potential

## Problem Statement

Charges of $+1\text{C}, -2\text{C}, +3\text{C}, -4\text{C}$ are placed at the corners of a square of side $1.0 \text{ m}$. Determine the electric potential at the center.

## Theory

Electric potential due to a point charge is

$$
V = k \frac{q}{r}
$$

Potential is scalar, so contributions add algebraically.

## Step-by-Step Solution

Distance from center to each corner:

$$
r = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}
$$

Total potential:

$$
V = k \frac{1 - 2 + 3 - 4}{r}
$$

$$
V = k \frac{-2}{r}
$$

Substitute $r$:

$$
V = -2k \sqrt{2}
$$

## Final Result

$$
V = -2\sqrt{2}k
$$

## Interpretation

Unlike force, potential does not cancel by symmetry unless charges sum to zero.
