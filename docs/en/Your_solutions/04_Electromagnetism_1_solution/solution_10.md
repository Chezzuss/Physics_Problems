# Task 10 – Force on Current-Carrying Wire

## Problem Statement

Wire length $L = 2.0 \text{ m}$, current $I = 10 \text{ A}$, field $B = 0.5 \text{ T}$.

## Theory

$$
F = I L B \sin \theta
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

### a) $\theta = 90^\circ$

$$
F = 10 \cdot 2 \cdot 0.5 = 10 \text{ N}
$$

### b) $\theta = 45^\circ$

$$
F = 10 \cdot 2 \cdot 0.5 \cdot \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}
$$

$$
F \approx 7.07 \text{ N}
$$

### c) $\theta = 0^\circ$

$$
F = 0
$$

## Final Result

- $90^\circ$: $10 \text{ N}$
- $45^\circ$: $7.07 \text{ N}$
- $0^\circ$: $0$

## Interpretation

Force depends on orientation relative to magnetic field.
