# Task 10 – Lorentz Force Acting on a Wire

## Problem Statement

A straight wire of length $L = 2.0 \text{ m}$ carries a current of $I = 10 \text{ A}$. It is placed in a uniform magnetic field of magnitude $B = 0.5 \text{ T}$. Determine the magnetic force on the wire for the following angles between the wire and the magnetic field:

a) $90^\circ$

b) $45^\circ$

c) $0^\circ$

## Theory

The magnitude of the magnetic force on a straight current-carrying wire is

$$
F = I L B \sin \theta
$$

where:

- $I$ is the current
- $L$ is the length of the wire in the field
- $B$ is the magnetic field magnitude
- $\theta$ is the angle between the wire and the magnetic field

## Step-by-Step Solution

First compute the constant factor:

$$
ILB = (10)(2.0)(0.5) = 10
$$

Thus,

$$
F = 10 \sin \theta
$$

### Case a: $\theta = 90^\circ$

Since

$$
\sin 90^\circ = 1
$$

the force is

$$
F = 10 \cdot 1 = 10 \text{ N}
$$

### Case b: $\theta = 45^\circ$

Since

$$
\sin 45^\circ = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}
$$

the force is

$$
F = 10 \cdot \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}
$$

$$
F = 5\sqrt{2} \text{ N}
$$

Numerically,

$$
F \approx 7.07 \text{ N}
$$

### Case c: $\theta = 0^\circ$

Since

$$
\sin 0^\circ = 0
$$

the force is

$$
F = 10 \cdot 0 = 0
$$

## Final Result

For the three cases:

a)

$$
F = 10 \text{ N}
$$

b)

$$
F = 5\sqrt{2} \text{ N} \approx 7.07 \text{ N}
$$

c)

$$
F = 0
$$

## Interpretation

The magnetic force is greatest when the wire is perpendicular to the magnetic field and vanishes when the wire is parallel to the field. Only the component of the wire perpendicular to the field contributes to the force.
