# Task 01 – Coulomb's Law

## Problem Statement

Four point charges of $+1.0 \text{ C}$ are placed at the corners of a square of side $1.0 \text{ m}$. A charge of $-2.0 \text{ C}$ is placed at the center. Determine the magnitude and direction of the net electric force acting on the central charge.

## Theory

The electric force between two point charges is given by Coulomb’s law:

$$
F = k \frac{|q_1 q_2|}{r^2}
$$

where

- $k = \frac{1}{4\pi \varepsilon_0}$
- $r$ is the distance between charges

The force is a vector, and the net force is obtained by vector summation of all contributions.

## Step-by-Step Solution

The four charges are symmetrically placed at the corners of a square.

The distance from the center to each corner is

$$
r = \frac{a}{\sqrt{2}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}
$$

Each corner charge exerts an attractive force on the central negative charge.

Due to symmetry:

- Forces from opposite corners cancel each other
- All horizontal and vertical components cancel pairwise

Thus, the net force is zero.

## Final Result

$$
F_{\text{net}} = 0
$$

## Interpretation

Although each charge exerts a force, perfect symmetry ensures complete cancellation. The center of the square is an equilibrium point.
