# Task 07 – Cyclotron Motion

## Problem Statement

An electron is accelerated from rest through a potential difference of $5000 \text{ V}$. It then enters a region of uniform magnetic field $B = 0.1 \text{ T}$ perpendicular to its velocity. Determine the radius of the circular path.

## Theory

When a charged particle is accelerated through a potential difference $V$, its gained kinetic energy is

$$
qV = \frac{1}{2}mv^2
$$

When the particle enters a magnetic field perpendicular to its velocity, the magnetic force provides the centripetal force:

$$
qvB = \frac{mv^2}{r}
$$

From this relation,

$$
r = \frac{mv}{qB}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

### Velocity of the electron after acceleration

The electron starts from rest, so all gained electric potential energy becomes kinetic energy:

$$
eV = \frac{1}{2}m_e v^2
$$

Solve for $v$:

$$
v = \sqrt{\frac{2eV}{m_e}}
$$

Use:

$$
e = 1.60 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}
$$

$$
m_e = 9.11 \times 10^{-31} \text{ kg}
$$

$$
V = 5000 \text{ V}
$$

Substitute:

$$
v = \sqrt{\frac{2(1.60 \times 10^{-19})(5000)}{9.11 \times 10^{-31}}}
$$

$$
v = \sqrt{1.756 \times 10^{15}}
$$

$$
v \approx 4.19 \times 10^7 \text{ m/s}
$$

### Radius of the circular path

Use

$$
r = \frac{m_e v}{eB}
$$

Substitute:

$$
r = \frac{(9.11 \times 10^{-31})(4.19 \times 10^7)}{(1.60 \times 10^{-19})(0.1)}
$$

$$
r \approx 2.38 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m}
$$

## Final Result

The radius of the circular trajectory is

$$
r \approx 2.4 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m}
$$

or

$$
r \approx 2.4 \text{ mm}
$$

## Interpretation

A stronger magnetic field would decrease the radius, while a greater accelerating voltage would increase the particle speed and therefore increase the radius.
