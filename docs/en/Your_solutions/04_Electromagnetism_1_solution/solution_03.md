# Task 03 – Electrostatic Equilibrium

## Problem Statement

Find the equilibrium position for a charge $q_3 = +1\text{C}$ placed between charges $q_1 = +4\text{C}$ and $q_2 = +9\text{C}$ separated by $2 \text{ m}$.

## Theory

Equilibrium occurs when net force is zero:

$$
F_1 = F_2
$$

Using Coulomb’s law:

$$
k \frac{q_1 q_3}{x^2} = k \frac{q_2 q_3}{(2 - x)^2}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

Cancel constants:

$$
\frac{4}{x^2} = \frac{9}{(2-x)^2}
$$

Take square root:

$$
\frac{2}{x} = \frac{3}{2-x}
$$

Solve:

$$
2(2-x) = 3x
$$

$$
4 - 2x = 3x
$$

$$
x = 0.8 \text{ m}
$$

## Final Result

$$
x = 0.8 \text{ m from } q_1
$$

## Interpretation

The equilibrium point lies closer to the smaller charge.
