# Task 06 – Energy Dissipation in Repeated Bounces

## Problem Statement

A tennis ball is dropped from a height of $2.0 \text{ m}$. After each bounce, it loses $30\%$ of its mechanical energy.

Determine the height reached after the second bounce.

## Theory

The gravitational potential energy of a ball at height $h$ is

$$
U = mgh
$$

If the ball loses $30\%$ of its mechanical energy at each bounce, then after each bounce it retains $70\%$ of the energy it had just before impact.

Thus, after each bounce,

$$
E_{\text{after}} = 0.7 E_{\text{before}}
$$

Since the maximum rebound height is proportional to the mechanical energy,

$$
h_{\text{new}} = 0.7 h_{\text{old}}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Initial height

The initial drop height is

$$
h_0 = 2.0 \text{ m}
$$

---

### 2. Height after the first bounce

The ball retains $70\%$ of its energy, so

$$
h_1 = 0.7 h_0
$$

$$
h_1 = 0.7 \cdot 2.0 = 1.4 \text{ m}
$$

---

### 3. Height after the second bounce

Again, the ball retains $70\%$ of the energy from the previous fall, so

$$
h_2 = 0.7 h_1
$$

$$
h_2 = 0.7 \cdot 1.4 = 0.98 \text{ m}
$$

Alternatively,

$$
h_2 = 2.0 \cdot 0.7^2
$$

$$
h_2 = 2.0 \cdot 0.49 = 0.98 \text{ m}
$$

## Final Result

After the second bounce, the ball rises to

$$
h_2 = 0.98 \text{ m}
$$

## Interpretation

Each bounce reduces the ball's energy by the same fraction. Therefore the rebound heights form a geometric sequence.

The heights decrease rapidly because the same percentage loss is applied repeatedly.
