# Task 05 – Inelastic Collision of a Runner and a Cart

## Problem Statement

A $70 \text{ kg}$ runner moving at $3 \text{ m/s}$ jumps onto a $140 \text{ kg}$ stationary cart.

Determine:

- the final speed of the cart with the runner,
- whether kinetic energy is conserved.

## Theory

When two bodies stick together after collision, the collision is perfectly inelastic.

Linear momentum is conserved:

$$
m_1 v_1 + m_2 v_2 = (m_1 + m_2)v_f
$$

Kinetic energy is generally not conserved in a perfectly inelastic collision, because part of it is transformed into heat, sound, and internal deformation.

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Apply conservation of momentum

Given:

$$
m_1 = 70 \text{ kg}
$$

$$
v_1 = 3 \text{ m/s}
$$

$$
m_2 = 140 \text{ kg}
$$

$$
v_2 = 0
$$

The final common speed is

$$
v_f = \frac{m_1 v_1 + m_2 v_2}{m_1 + m_2}
$$

Substitute values:

$$
v_f = \frac{70 \cdot 3 + 140 \cdot 0}{70 + 140}
$$

$$
v_f = \frac{210}{210}
$$

$$
v_f = 1 \text{ m/s}
$$

---

### 2. Check kinetic energy before the collision

Initial kinetic energy:

$$
K_i = \frac{1}{2}m_1 v_1^2 + \frac{1}{2}m_2 v_2^2
$$

Since $v_2=0$,

$$
K_i = \frac{1}{2} \cdot 70 \cdot 3^2
$$

$$
K_i = 35 \cdot 9 = 315 \text{ J}
$$

---

### 3. Check kinetic energy after the collision

Final kinetic energy:

$$
K_f = \frac{1}{2}(m_1+m_2)v_f^2
$$

$$
K_f = \frac{1}{2} \cdot 210 \cdot 1^2
$$

$$
K_f = 105 \text{ J}
$$

---

### 4. Compare energies

$$
K_i = 315 \text{ J}
$$

$$
K_f = 105 \text{ J}
$$

Since

$$
K_f \neq K_i
$$

kinetic energy is not conserved.

The loss is

$$
\Delta K = 315 - 105 = 210 \text{ J}
$$

## Final Result

The final speed of the cart with the runner is

$$
v_f = 1.0 \text{ m/s}
$$

Kinetic energy is not conserved.

## Interpretation

Momentum is conserved because there is no net external horizontal force during the collision.

Kinetic energy decreases because the runner and cart stick together. Part of the original mechanical energy is converted into non-mechanical forms such as heat, sound, and internal deformation.
